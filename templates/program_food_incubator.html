About the Incubator

The Food Business Incubator provides aspiring food entrepreneurs with the facilities, knowledge, and support needed to launch a successful food business on Staten Island. Our program addresses the biggest barriers to entry: access to licensed commercial kitchen space, food safety certifications, and business guidance specific to the food industry.

Whether you're launching a food truck, starting a catering business, or producing specialty products for retail, our 6-month incubator program gives you the resources and mentorship to turn your culinary passion into a thriving business.

What's Included

Commercial Kitchen Access

  • Fully licensed and equipped commercial kitchen facility
  • Flexible scheduling with day, evening, and weekend slots
  • Professional-grade equipment: ovens, stoves, mixers, prep stations
  • Refrigeration and dry storage space
  • Shared access to packaging and labeling equipment

Training & Certifications

  • NYC Food Protection Certificate prep course and exam
  • ServSafe Manager certification training
  • Allergen awareness and proper food labeling
  • HACCP principles and food safety protocols
  • Kitchen sanitation and equipment maintenance

Business Development

  • One-on-one business planning and strategy sessions
  • Food costing, recipe scaling, and pricing guidance
  • Marketing, branding, and social media for food businesses
  • NYC health department permits and inspection preparation
  • Supplier sourcing and vendor relationship building
  • Sales channels: farmers markets, retail, wholesale, online

Networking & Mentorship

  • Monthly cohort meetings and peer learning sessions
  • Mentorship from successful food business owners
  • Connections to food distributors, retailers, and event organizers
  • Participation in local food festivals and farmers markets

Ideal For These Food Businesses

  • Food Trucks & Carts: Prep work and production when you're not on the road
  • Catering Services: Kitchen space for events and advance preparation
  • Specialty Foods: Sauces, baked goods, prepared meals for retail sale
  • Meal Prep Services: Bulk cooking and packaging for delivery or pickup
  • Pop-Up Concepts: Test and refine recipes before opening a restaurant
  • Artisan Products: Small-batch jams, sauces, baked goods, confections

Program Timeline

Month 1: Foundation

  • Complete food safety certifications
  • Orient to kitchen facilities and equipment
  • Finalize business concept and target market
  • Begin permit and licensing process

Months 2-3: Development

  • Recipe testing and standardization
  • Food costing and menu pricing
  • Branding, packaging, and marketing materials
  • Identify sales channels and distribution strategy

Months 4-5: Launch Preparation

  • Ramp up production and build inventory
  • Secure initial customers or retail partners
  • Soft launch at farmers markets or events
  • Gather customer feedback and refine offerings

Month 6: Launch & Transition

  • Official business launch
  • Graduate showcase event with local food industry
  • Transition plan for continued kitchen access (if needed)
  • Connect with ongoing support resources

Who Should Apply

This program is designed for:

  • Aspiring food entrepreneurs with a clear business concept
  • Home cooks ready to commercialize their recipes
  • Culinary school graduates looking to start their own business
  • Existing food businesses needing compliant kitchen space
Commitment Required: This is an intensive program. We're looking for serious entrepreneurs ready to dedicate significant time to building their business over 6 months.

Graduate Success Stories

Island Spice Kitchen - Launched Caribbean catering business through the incubator, now operates a food truck and supplies three local restaurants with signature hot sauces.

Sweet Starts Bakery - Developed wholesale cookie and pastry business, now distributed in 15 cafes and specialty shops across Staten Island and Brooklyn.

Fresh Meal Prep SI - Built healthy meal prep delivery service from the ground up, now serving 200+ weekly customers with plans to open a storefront.

After the Incubator

Graduates can:

  • Continue renting kitchen time on an hourly or monthly basis
  • Join our food business alumni network for ongoing support
  • Access additional business development workshops
  • Participate in Staten Island food events and markets
  • Mentor future incubator cohorts
