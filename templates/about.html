🎯 What This Platform Does

Data Driven Staten Island transforms raw public economic data into clear, actionable visuals. It aggregates employment statistics, small business dynamics, transit ridership, and rental markets — all scoped specifically to Staten Island's Richmond County — so any resident or business owner can make informed decisions without needing a data science background.

👥 Who It's Built For
  • Small business owners tracking local market conditions and labor trends
  • Job seekers & students exploring local employment opportunities
  • Community organizations referencing borough-level statistics for grant reports
  • Researchers & educators studying Staten Island's economic landscape
  • Policymakers who need accessible local data to support decisions

Data Sources & Methodology
How the numbers are sourced and what they represent
Employment & Unemployment
Official Labor Statistics
  • Geography: Richmond County, NY (Staten Island)
  • Annual unemployment rates from NYS Department of Labor LAUS and FRED series NYRICH5URN
  • Employment rate approximated as 100 – unemployment rate per year for visualization
  • Refreshed annually when new LAUS / ACS releases become available
Small Business Dynamics
Derived / Calibrated
  • Approximate count of small businesses opening and closing per year in Staten Island
  • Calibrated to NYC SBS reports and NYS DOL QCEW sector data
  • Exact counts not published in a single open dataset — values demonstrate dashboard behavior with real Chamber or QCEW feeds
  • Production deployment would use automated pulls from official sources
Transit Ridership
MTA / NYC Open Data
  • Staten Island Ferry, SIR, Express Bus, and Local Bus ridership
  • Fetched from NYC Open Data ferry ridership endpoint; annual totals aggregated from daily/monthly records
  • Mode splits based on published MTA annual report ratios
  • Falls back to calibrated historical estimates when API is unavailable
Median Rent
HUD / Zillow / StreetEasy
  • Attempts live pull from HUD Fair Market Rent API and NYC Housing datasets
  • Fallback data calibrated from Zillow Rent Index, StreetEasy median rent reports, and NYC HPD rent guideline reports
  • Represents median monthly rent for Richmond County residential units
Update Strategy
How data stays current

In this prototype, data files are updated manually at least once per year using the most recent ACS and New York State Department of Labor releases. On each application startup, the system attempts live API calls to all configured endpoints — if they succeed, the database is populated with real data; if they fail, calibrated fallback data derived from official published sources is used instead.

In a future production iteration, the Flask API endpoints (/api/employment, /api/business, /api/transit, /api/rent) would connect directly to Census, NY DOL, MTA, and HUD APIs to automate monthly or annual refreshes without manual intervention.
