About this dashboard

Data Driven Staten Island is a prototype application decision‑support dashboard developed as a capstone project for the Verrazzano Honors Program and ISI 490 at the College of Staten Island. It aggregates publicly available statistics and locally curated information to help Staten Island stakeholders understand employment and small‑business dynamics.

Data sources & update schedule

Employment & unemployment (labor chart)
Source: official labor statistics
  • Geography: Richmond County, NY (Staten Island).
  • Annual unemployment rates derived from New York State Department of Labor Local Area Unemployment Statistics and the FRED series “Unemployment Rate in Richmond County, NY (NYRICH5URN)”.
  • Employment rate in the chart is approximated as 100 minus the unemployment rate for each year, used for visualization purposes.
  • Data is stored in employment_data.json and can be refreshed each year when new LAUS / ACS releases become available.
Small business dynamics (business chart)
Source: derived / illustrative
  • Metric represents the approximate number of small businesses opening and closing per year in Staten Island, calibrated to trends described in NYC small‑business reports and NYS DOL QCEW sector data.
  • Exact counts are not published in a single open dataset; the values in business_data.json are used to demonstrate how the dashboard would behave with real Chamber or QCEW feeds.
  • In a production deployment, these figures would be replaced with automated pulls from official economic development or Chamber data sources.

Update strategy

In this prototype, JSON files are updated manually at least once per year using the most recent ACS and New York State Department of Labor releases. In a future iteration, the Flask API endpoints (/api/employment and /api/business) could be connected directly to Census and NY DOL APIs to automate refreshes on a monthly or annual basis.
