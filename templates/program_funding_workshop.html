Small Business Funding & Grants Workshop

Learn about SBA loans, microloans, crowdfunding, and grant opportunities for Staten Island businesses. Get help with applications and connect with local lenders and funding organizations.

💰 Financing 📋 Application Support 🤝 Lender Connections 💼 Grant Opportunities

About the Workshop

Securing funding is one of the biggest challenges for small business owners. Our quarterly Funding & Grants Workshop demystifies the financing process and connects Staten Island entrepreneurs with resources, programs, and people who can help.

Whether you're looking for startup capital, working capital for growth, or grant funding for specific initiatives, this workshop provides practical knowledge and direct connections to funding sources.

What You'll Learn

Understanding Funding Options

  • Overview of SBA 7(a) and 504 loan programs
  • Microloans for businesses needing smaller amounts
  • Equipment financing and lines of credit
  • Crowdfunding platforms and strategies
  • Angel investors and venture capital basics
  • Friends and family funding considerations

Grant Opportunities

  • NYC small business grants and relief programs
  • Industry-specific grants (retail, food, tech, nonprofit)
  • Minority-owned and women-owned business grants
  • Green business and sustainability grants
  • Community development and neighborhood improvement grants

Preparing Your Application

  • Building a strong business plan and financial projections
  • Understanding credit scores and financial statements
  • Personal guarantee and collateral requirements
  • Documentation needed for different funding sources
  • Common application mistakes and how to avoid them

Navigating the Process

  • Typical timeline from application to funding
  • Working with loan officers and underwriters
  • Negotiating terms and understanding agreements
  • Managing debt and building business credit

Workshop Format

Each 3-hour workshop includes:

  • Expert Presentations: Hear from SBA representatives, local lenders, and grant administrators
  • Panel Discussion: Business owners share their funding journeys and lessons learned
  • Breakout Sessions: Small group discussions by funding type or business stage
  • One-on-One Consultations: 15-minute sessions with lenders and advisors (sign up at the workshop)
  • Resource Fair: Meet representatives from funding organizations and support programs

Who Should Attend

This workshop is valuable for:

  • Entrepreneurs planning to start a business
  • Existing business owners looking to expand or renovate
  • Business owners facing cash flow challenges
  • Anyone interested in understanding small business financing
No financial background required! We explain concepts in plain language and provide resources to help you at every stage of the funding process. Come with questions - we're here to help.

Featured Partners

Representatives from these organizations regularly attend our workshops:

  • U.S. Small Business Administration (SBA) - Federal loan and grant programs
  • NYC Department of Small Business Services - Local grants and incentives
  • Community Development Financial Institutions (CDFIs) - Mission-driven lenders
  • Local Banks & Credit Unions - Traditional lending options
  • SCORE - Free mentoring and business advising
  • Staten Island Chamber of Commerce - Member resources and advocacy

Success Stories

Rosa's Bakery - Attended workshop, connected with a CDFI lender, and secured a $50,000 microloan to purchase commercial ovens and expand production capacity.

Island Construction Services - Learned about equipment financing at the workshop and financed new vehicles and tools, improving efficiency and client reach.

Tech Start SI - Discovered a minority-owned business grant program and received $25,000 for marketing and hiring their first employee.

Before You Attend

To make the most of your time at the workshop, consider bringing:

  • Your business plan or concept description
  • Recent financial statements (if business is operating)
  • Questions about specific funding needs
  • Notebook for taking notes
  • Business cards for networking

Note: You don't need to have these materials to attend - everyone is welcome!
